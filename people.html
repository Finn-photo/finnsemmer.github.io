About
The photographer

From mountains
to human faces

I started out as a landscape photographer, drawn to the raw beauty of mountains, deep forests, and vast coastlines. But living in Berlin, a city of concrete and glass, I soon realized that my surroundings didn't match my initial vision. Instead of searching for landscapes, I turned my lens toward people.

What fascinates me most is the interplay between individuals and their urban environment — the contrast between soft expressions and sharp architecture, natural emotions in a structured setting. Photography, for me, is more than just taking pictures. It's connecting with people. It's a dialogue, a glimpse into someone's world.
